# U.S. Data Scientist Market Analysis — Project Summary

**Dataset:** 742 U.S. job postings from a Glassdoor scrape published on Kaggle  
**Scope:** Salary, geography, employer characteristics, and tool demand

---

## Project Context

This project was completed as part of graduate coursework in analytics and visualization. It uses a public job-posting dataset to examine how the 2021 U.S. data-science market was structured across salary, location, organization profile, and technical requirements.

The main question was: what patterns become visible when salary, geography, and skill demand are explored together rather than in isolation?

---

## What Was Done

- Cleaned and standardized the job-posting dataset
- Verified summary statistics across multiple tools
- Built a Power BI dashboard with multiple views of salary, geography, company profile, and tool demand
- Used dashboard filters and Key Influencers analysis to compare role types and degree/seniority patterns
- Documented the workflow and interpretation in a written report

---

## Main Takeaways

- **Python dominated the market.** It appeared as the most consistently requested technical tool across job types.
- **Coastal markets were the strongest hubs.** California, Massachusetts, and New York showed the most visible concentration of role volume and salary.
- **Employer profiles were not random.** Many postings came from large, private organizations concentrated in IT and biotech-related sectors.
- **Degree expectations increased with role level.** More senior and research-oriented roles showed stronger MA and PhD requirements.
- **The dashboard format mattered.** The project works not just as a static report, but as a market-exploration tool that lets users examine patterns across multiple dimensions.
