# Employee Engagement Survey Design

This project develops a quarterly employee engagement pulse survey from the ground up using the Utrecht Work Engagement Scale as its conceptual foundation. The emphasis is on measurement design rather than analysis alone, which makes the project useful as a demonstration of survey methodology, construct-driven item selection, and implementation planning.

The design covers the full survey lifecycle: theoretical framework, item structure, demographic questions, sampling strategy, administration guidance, scoring, reporting, and planned validation steps. That breadth is what makes the project stronger than a simple questionnaire draft. It shows how survey design decisions affect data quality before any analysis begins.

One of the clearest strengths of the project is its connection to later psychometric work. The design does not treat the survey as complete once items are written; it explicitly plans for pilot testing, reliability review, factor analysis, and confirmatory validation before any full production use.

As a portfolio piece, this project demonstrates research design, organizational measurement, and survey-development thinking in a way that complements the more quantitative modeling projects elsewhere in the portfolio.
