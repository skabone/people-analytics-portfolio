# America's Broadband Problem
**Tools:** Python · RapidMiner · Tableau | **Domain:** Public Policy Analytics / Equity Research

---

## Overview

A county-level analysis of U.S. broadband access inequality using merged data from IMLS, BroadbandNow, and the FCC (3,102 counties). Examines which counties are most at risk of being left behind — and whether Black American communities face disparate broadband outcomes.

## Key Findings

- **The broadband gap is real** — county-level data reveals dramatic variation that national averages conceal
- **Poverty rate beats FCC data** as a predictor of actual broadband access — official metrics overstate coverage due to flawed operationalization
- **North is better than South; East coast is mixed** — geographic gradients in access, speed, and cost run along predictable latitude/longitude lines
- **Two Americas pattern** — the majority of U.S. residents live in a small number of dense coastal metros; the majority of U.S. counties are rural with far worse broadband outcomes
- **Disparate impact on Black American communities** — counties with the highest Black American population concentrations are disproportionately represented among the worst-served
