# ANOVA Methods Collection

This project brings together four common ANOVA designs and applies each to a different simulated research scenario. The purpose is not simply to run four tests, but to show how research design determines the correct ANOVA structure, the relevant assumptions, and the interpretation of the result.

The collection is useful because it highlights contrasts that often get blurred together in practice. A one-way ANOVA answers a different class of question from a factorial ANOVA, and repeated-measures or mixed designs add still another layer by modeling within-person change over time. Seeing them side by side makes the logic of each design easier to understand.

One of the strongest themes in the collection is that interaction effects and design structure matter as much as the omnibus p-value. The factorial and mixed-design examples are especially helpful because they show why main effects alone are often not the most interesting result.

As a portfolio piece, this project demonstrates statistical design fluency, ANOVA assumption awareness, and the ability to organize several related analytical methods into one coherent reference-style package.
